# Balsamic Italian Pasta Salad

A big-batch balsamic Italian pasta salad with penne, roasted red peppers, and Parmesan. Oil and vinegar dressing, no mayo. Gets better as it sits.

**Servings:** 12
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**Rest Time:** 1 hour (overnight is better)

---

## Ingredients

### Pasta
- 2 lb penne pasta
- 2 tbsp kosher salt (for pasta water)

### Dressing
- 1/3 cup balsamic vinegar
- 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 2/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried basil
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
- 1 tsp honey or sugar
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Mix-ins
- 12 oz roasted red peppers (jarred), drained and chopped
- 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, shaved or shredded
- 1/3 cup fresh basil, torn
- 1/3 cup fresh parsley, chopped

---

## Instructions

### 1. Cook the pasta
Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the kosher salt. Cook the penne according to package directions until al dente — you want it slightly firm since it will soften as it sits in the dressing. Drain and do **not** rinse.

### 2. Make the dressing
While the pasta cooks, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, apple cider vinegar, olive oil, Dijon mustard, oregano, basil, garlic powder, red pepper flakes, and honey until emulsified. Season with salt and pepper. Taste and adjust the vinegar-to-oil ratio to your liking.

### 3. Dress the warm pasta
Transfer the hot drained pasta to a large bowl. Pour about 3/4 of the dressing over the warm pasta and toss well. The warm penne will absorb the dressing into the tubes. Reserve the remaining dressing. Let cool for about **15 minutes**.

### 4. Add the mix-ins
Once the pasta has cooled a bit, add the roasted red peppers and Parmesan. Toss to combine.

### 5. Chill and finish
Refrigerate for at least **1 hour** (overnight is even better). Before serving, toss with the reserved dressing and add the fresh basil and parsley. Taste for seasoning — the pasta will have absorbed a lot, so don't be shy about adding a splash more balsamic or olive oil if it looks dry.
