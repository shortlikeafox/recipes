# Balsamic Mustard Potato Salad

A tangy, vinegar-forward potato salad with whole grain mustard, fresh herbs, and a nice kick from dill peppers. No mayo in sight.

**Servings:** 6
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**Rest Time:** 20 minutes - 1 hour

---

## Ingredients

### Potatoes
- 3 lb russet potatoes, cut into 3/4-inch cubes
- 1 tbsp kosher salt (for boiling water)

### Vinaigrette
- 3 tbsp balsamic vinegar
- 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 2 tbsp whole grain mustard
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp honey or maple syrup
- 1 tbsp pepperoncini brine (from the jar)
- Salt and black pepper to taste

### Mix-ins
- 1/2 cup dill peppers (pepperoncini), sliced
- 1/2 red onion, finely diced
- 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh chives, sliced

---

## Instructions

### 1. Boil the potatoes
Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover with cold water by about an inch. Add the kosher salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook until fork-tender but not falling apart, about **12-15 minutes**. Russets break down faster than waxy potatoes, so check early.

### 2. Make the vinaigrette
While the potatoes cook, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, apple cider vinegar, whole grain mustard, Dijon mustard, olive oil, honey, and pepperoncini brine in a large bowl until emulsified. Season with salt and pepper.

### 3. Dress the warm potatoes
Drain the potatoes well and let them steam dry for a minute or two. While still warm, gently fold them into the vinaigrette. The warm potatoes will absorb the dressing much better than cold ones. Let them sit for about **10 minutes**.

### 4. Add the crunch and herbs
Gently fold in the pepperoncini, red onion, parsley, and chives. Toss carefully so the potatoes don't completely break apart (a few broken edges are fine and help thicken the dressing).

### 5. Rest and adjust
Let the salad sit at room temperature for at least **20 minutes**, or refrigerate for an hour or more. Taste and adjust seasoning before serving. The potatoes will soak up dressing as they cool, so you may want a splash more balsamic or olive oil before serving.

---

## Notes

- **Dress while warm** -- this is the key move. Warm potatoes absorb so much more flavor.
- **Russets are a feature, not a bug** -- they break down a bit more than red or Yukon Gold potatoes, which actually works in your favor. The starchy edges thicken the vinaigrette into a creamy coating without any mayo.
- **Three layers of acid** -- the balsamic, apple cider vinegar, and pepperoncini brine each bring something different. The balsamic adds depth, the ACV adds mellow fruitiness, and the brine adds tangy heat.
- For more crunch, add a handful of thinly sliced radishes.
- To lean even more tangy, add a tablespoon of capers.
